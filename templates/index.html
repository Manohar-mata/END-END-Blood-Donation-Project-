Why Blood Donation is Important

Blood donation is a vital process that helps save lives. Every two seconds, someone in the U.S. needs blood. Donating blood can help patients suffering from cancer, traumatic injuries, chronic illnesses, and surgeries. Your donation can make a significant difference in someone's life.

What to Expect During the Donation Process

Donating blood is a simple and safe process. Here’s what you can expect:

  • Registration: You will complete a donor registration form with your personal details.
  • Health History and Mini-Physical: A staff member will ask about your health history and perform a quick physical check-up.
  • Donation: The actual blood donation takes about 10-15 minutes. You will be comfortably seated during the process.
  • Refreshments: After donating, you will be given snacks and drinks to help your body recover.

Check Whether You Are Eligible to Donate Blood or Not

Before you donate blood, it's important to ensure that you meet the eligibility requirements. Here are some common criteria:

  • Age: You must be at least 17 years old (16 with parental consent in some states).
  • Weight: You must weigh at least 110 pounds.
  • Health: You must be in good health and feeling well on the day of donation.
  • Travel: Certain travel history may affect your eligibility.
  • Medications: Some medications may affect your eligibility.
